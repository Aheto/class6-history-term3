📘 Presidents of the Fourth Republic

💡 Introduction

Since the birth of the Fourth Republic in 1993, Ghana has had five presidents. These presidents have come from two major political parties: the National Democratic Congress (NDC) and the New Patriotic Party (NPP). The peaceful transfer of power between these parties is a sign of Ghana's strong democracy.

🇬🇭 1. Flight Lieutenant Jerry Rawlings (1993–2001)

  • Political Party: National Democratic Congress (NDC)
  • Background: Before becoming a civilian president, he led the PNDC military government. He resigned from the military in 1992 to contest and win the 1992 elections.
  • Key Fact: He served two terms (8 years) and peacefully handed over power to his main opponent in 2001.

🇬🇭 2. John Agyekum Kufuor (2001–2009)

  • Political Party: New Patriotic Party (NPP)
  • Background: A seasoned politician and lawyer who previously held ministerial positions.
  • Key Fact: He served two terms (8 years) and was known for significant economic reforms and infrastructure development.

🇬🇭 3. Professor John Atta Mills (2009–2012)

  • Political Party: National Democratic Congress (NDC)
  • Background: A brilliant academic and tax law expert who previously served as Vice President under Rawlings.
  • Key Fact: Tragically, he died in office on 24th July 2012 at the age of 68.

🇬🇭 4. John Dramani Mahama (2012–2017)

  • Political Party: National Democratic Congress (NDC)
  • Background: He was the Vice President under John Atta Mills.
  • Key Fact: He was sworn in immediately after the death of President Mills, completing the remaining term and then contesting in the 2012 elections.

🇬🇭 5. Nana Addo Dankwa Akufo-Addo (2017–present)

  • Political Party: New Patriotic Party (NPP)
  • Background: A prominent lawyer, former Member of Parliament, and former Minister of Foreign Affairs.
  • Key Fact: He won the 2016 elections and took office in January 2017, marking the NPP's return to power.
💭 Think About It: Why is the peaceful transfer of power from one political party to another (like from NDC to NPP and back) so important for a country's development and stability?
