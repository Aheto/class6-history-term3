📚 Class 6 History

The Political Parties of the Fourth Republic

Indicator: B6.6.1.1.2

Lesson Objectives

Master the key concepts of Ghana's Fourth Republic through four focused lessons.

Lesson 1 B6.6.1.1.1

Events Leading to the Fourth Republic

Strand: Independent Ghana  |  Sub-strand: The Republics

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Define what a republic is and explain Ghana's journey through four republics
  • Describe the military coups d'état that interrupted constitutional rule (1966, 1972, 1979, 1981)
  • Explain the events that led to the emergence of the Fourth Republic in 1993
  • Identify the role of Flight Lieutenant Jerry Rawlings in the transition to multi-party democracy
  • Discuss the writing of the 1992 Constitution and its significance
  • Sequence the timeline of republics and military rule from 1960 to present
Core Competencies: Communication & Collaboration, Critical Thinking, Personal Development & Leadership
RepublicCoup d'étatMulti-party democracyConstitutionPNDCTransition
Lesson 2 B6.6.1.1.2

Political Parties of the Fourth Republic

Strand: Independent Ghana  |  Sub-strand: The Republics

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Identify the two major political parties: NDC and NPP
  • Name the five presidents of the Fourth Republic and their political parties
  • Match each president to their years in office (Rawlings 1993-2001, Kufuor 2001-2009, Mills 2009-2012, Mahama 2012-2017, Akufo-Addo 2017-present)
  • Recognize other registered political parties (GCPP, APC, RPD, YPP, DFP, etc.)
  • Explain the concept of peaceful transfer of power through democratic elections
  • Describe the inauguration ceremony and the Sword of Authority
Core Competencies: Communication, Creativity & Innovation, Cultural Identity
NDCNPPPresidentInaugurationElectionsDemocracyFlagbearerCabinet
Lesson 3 B6.6.2.1.1

Military Regimes and Coup Leaders

Strand: Independent Ghana  |  Sub-strand: Military Rule

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Identify the six military coups d'état in Ghana's history
  • Name the leaders of each coup: Joseph Ankrah (1966), Ignatius Acheampong (1972), Jerry Rawlings (1979 & 1981), Fred Akuffo (1978)
  • Match military regimes to their names: NLC, SMC, AFRC, PNDC
  • Explain the reasons given for each military takeover
  • Describe how military rule interrupted constitutional governance
  • Discuss why the Fourth Republic has remained stable without military intervention
Core Competencies: Critical Thinking, Personal Development, Citizenship
Military regimeCoup d'étatNLCSMCAFRCPNDCOverthrowDictatorship
Lesson 4 B6.6.2.2.2

Consequences of Military Takeovers

Strand: Independent Ghana  |  Sub-strand: Military Rule

By the end of this lesson, learners will be able to:

  • Assess the negative effects: economic decline, human rights violations, loss of lives, insecurity
  • Identify positive outcomes: accountability, law and order, awareness of people's interests
  • Explain how coups prevented political and economic development
  • Discuss the violation of human rights and confiscation of property
  • Analyze why democratic governance is preferable to military rule
  • Conclude whether military takeovers helped or harmed Ghana's development
Core Competencies: Critical Thinking, Problem Solving, Citizenship
Human rightsEconomic developmentInsecurityCurfewInvestorsDemocracy

Differentiation Strategies

Tailored support to ensure every learner succeeds — whether they need extra help or an extra challenge.

For Struggling Learners

  • Use flashcards and matching games (Resource 9, 11)
  • Pair work for peer support
  • Simplified timelines with key dates only
  • Remedial quizzes in pairs

For Advanced Learners

  • Research additional presidents' biographies
  • Write comparative essays on republics
  • Create detailed timelines with photos
  • Interview community leaders about governance
